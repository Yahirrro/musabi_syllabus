Reflect on visual messages in discussions with fellow students, acquire communication skills necessary for their practice. The students are encouraged to question their usual thinking patterns, reconsider them, and gain new knowledge from them.Cross-cultural awareness training in the field of art and design and thinking and speaking in english will be part of the goals for this course.*<BR/><BR/>The content and goals of this course might be modified and adjusted to the ability and needs of the participating students.  <tr bgcolor="#FFFFFF">          <td nowrap bgcolor="#99FFCC">                    			                        <font color="#000000">授業計画・課題に対するフィードバック</font>			                    </td>          <td colspan="3">                                                  Lesson 1<BR/>1) Teacher and students self-introduction in English– (Keeping the introduction short, informal, and creative)It can include: what inspires you / which designer, or artist you are interested in, and so on <BR/>2) Introduction to the outline of the course and explanation of the «main work». the students have to execute this work during the entire course(8 lessons), ending in a presentation.(As a first step the students have to choose one picture, from a selection of images provided by the teacher, the student will work with this picture through all the lessons.)– Describe the picture, describe what you see, describe what could have happened. In writing and recording and also in an interview form.Prepare the best story as your final presentation.– Design the book cover or movie poster for your story.<BR/>Lesson 2<BR/>1) What is storytelling? Introduction to the importance of story telling<BR/>2) Showing short film (one scene from different angles)Film: one scene seen from different angles tells different stories<BR/>3) Discussion<BR/>Lesson 3<BR/>1) How to search the Internet in EnglishIntroduction to different ways of doing Online research / why multilingual searches are opening the horizon.(Wikipedia in English, German and Japanese do most of the time not share the same information (dates, of course, are the same, but the focus on context and substance is different, it depends on the cultural background)<BR/>2) Group work «cadavre exquis» (creative story writing game)Sending a sentence by mail. Each student adds a new sentence, send’s it to the next, and deletes the old one, the teacher is always in cc)<BR/>3) Homework; take pictures in your neighborhood and/or at home of anyEnglish words or sentences and have them ready for the next lesson.(Show some samples)<BR/>Lesson 4<BR/>1) Organize your pictures into groups. Create a collage with your found word images and write a short explanation of the structure you have chosen.(The students are free to design, search for patterns or arrange by colour, content, letters, font, etc.)<BR/>2) Teacher-led discussion about the found word-images collages. Analyzing the typography, what it tells us in context to where they were found.<BR/>Lesson 5<BR/>1) Each student presents his/her finished collage to fellow students.Start discussion<BR/>2) The teacher will handout the texts for the next lesson for the students to prepare<BR/>Lesson 6<BR/>1) Reading a classic essay of a «modernist designer» (like Brockmann, Rand, Tschichold) in class and discuss.(The teacher will provide the essay, all the students have to read the same)<BR/>2) Discuss in class what this means today and if it is still relevant.<BR/>Lesson 7<BR/>1) Watching different films of well-known designers on how they present their work.<BR/>2) Discuss and analyze the presentation.<BR/>Lesson 8<BR/>1) Teacher-student one on one support and hands-on working on the students’«main work».<BR/>2) Review on the first 8 lessons, student and teacher discussion.                                      &nbsp;				             </td>        </tr>
